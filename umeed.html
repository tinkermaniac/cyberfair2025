About Umeed

Our Mission

उम्मीद सिर्फ एक शब्द नहीं, ये एक अहसास है जो हमें आगे बढ़ने की हिम्मत देता है।In a world that moves fast, it is easy to overlook the quiet voices of the elderly, whose lives are filled with stories of struggle, sacrifice, and triumph.

Umeed is here to give them the care, attention, and love they deserve, ensuring that their golden years are not filled with loneliness but are instead filled with warmth, dignity, and companionship. Umeed’s mission is to be a guiding light in their twilight years, telling them: “You are not alone. You are not forgotten.”

Who are we ?

We are a team of motivated students who are devoted to the care of their elderly and wish to embrace the positive values of Indian culture to become proud ambassadors of it. We are students of Aravali International School, Sec-43, Faridabad, Haryana, India.

Our Aim

अंधेरे के बादल कितने भी घने क्यों न हों, उम्मीद की किरण हमेशा रास्ता बना ही लेती है।

We have already collaborated with various organizations and institutions, bringing them on board as partners in this endeavor. As part of our initiative, we facilitate interactions between orphaned children and elderly residents, serving as a bridge between them and creating meaningful connections and shared experiences.

We aim to take this endeavour further and with a vision to expand our impact, we are in the process of developing a proposal to establish an NGO named 'UMEED'. This NGO will provide a shared home for both elderly individuals and orphaned children, fostering intergenerational bonding, emotional security, and a sense of belonging for all.

We aim to connect with numerous elderly individuals and empower the younger generation to become their support system. Additionally, we aim to bridge young adults and the parent community with old age homes, fostering meaningful relationships and providing much-needed support to the elderly.

Our Journey

January - March 2024

The foundation for Umeed was laid by identifying key areas in elderly care and developing an action plan. Initial visits to old age homes were conducted to understand their challenges and explore ways to build meaningful connections.

April - June 2024

Surveys and research were conducted to assess the5needs of seniors, shaping future initiatives. Visits to old age homes along with a new reach to nearby orphanages continued, fostering intergenerational bonding through interactive activities.

July - September 2024

The Vintage Veterans app was developed to provide seniors with companionship, medication reminders, and emotional support. On-ground activities, including visits and engagement programs, were carried out while surveys collected feedback for improvement.

October - December 2024

The success of past initiatives was evaluated through testimonials and feedback. Visits remained a core activity, ensuring continuous support, while future strategies were developed to enhance impact.

January - March 2025

The Umeed website has been designed to establish an online presence for the initiative. The Vintage Veterans app has been integrated into the platform to expand its accessibility. Visits to orphanages and old age homes have continued, reinforcing community engagement. An NGO proposal is being developed to formalize efforts and secure future collaborations.

Understanding Umeed

What is 'Umeed'?

Ummeed is a profound and essential human emotion, deeply intertwined with resilience, life's challenges, failures, and disappointments. It is the invisible force that allows individuals to persevere despite adversity, reminding them that hardships are temporary and that better days lie ahead.

Ultimately, hope is more than just wishful thinking; it is a force that keeps the world moving forward. It is what allows people to dream, to fight, and to believe in something greater than their present circumstances. As long as there is hope, there is the possibility of change, growth, and renewal.

Why 'Umeed'?

"To live without hope is to cease to live."

Fyodor Dostoyevsky

The title "Umeed" translates to "Hope" in Hindi, which perfectly reflects the mission and vision of this project. For the Team Umeed, it is a hope to be dedicated, to love and contribute selflessly to our community, and a hope to join hands for comprehensive change.

Through this initiative, we aim to bring hope and joy to elderly citizens who need it the most. The title adds a layer of cultural sensitivity, making the website more relatable and accessible to its target audience. Ultimately, it aligns perfectly with the website's values of providing support, comfort, resources, and hope to elderly individuals, making 'ummeed' a fitting and impactful choice.

Our Community

Description of Our Community

Our community is a reflection of India's deep-rooted cultural values, where love, respect, and care for the elderly are at the core of family life. In every corner of this diverse country, elders are regarded with the utmost reverence, their wisdom and presence seen as treasures.

The bond between the younger and older generations is at the core of our culture, ensuring that the elderly are never forgotten or overlooked. From the meals prepared with love to the stories shared over chai, our community thrives on compassion and respect for those who came before us. This sense of care extends beyond our homes, as we work together to create a world where every elder is valued and loved.

This is our community—a place where love for our elders is a tradition and a way of life.

Project Summary

'Umeed' is a Hindi word and, here, it is driven by a simple yet powerful goal: to create an opportunity for youngsters to fulfil the hopes of elderly individuals.

Thus, Umeed aims to be a one-stop platform for young individuals to connect with old age homes, visit the elderly, and engage them in meaningful activities. Rooted in the deep respect for elders that Indian traditions uphold, Umeed seeks to bridge the growing disconnect between generations and provide care, companionship, and engagement for senior citizens.

Extensive research, including visits to old age homes, has shed light on the challenges faced by the elderly, particularly the loneliness that often surrounds them. To address this, a variety of activities have been designed to keep them engaged while also enhancing their mental and physical well-being.

Beyond supporting senior citizens, Umeed is also working towards connecting orphanages with old age homes. Plans are underway to establish an NGO that will bring both orphans and the elderly under one roof—creating a nurturing environment filled with love, care, and companionship for all.
